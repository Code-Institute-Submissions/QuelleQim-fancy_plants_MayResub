Plant Care

Water Care

How much you should water your plants differ greatly depending on what kind of plant, soil, pot, humidity, temperature and amount of sun. A lot of variables to take in, but rest assure that when you figure out exactly what kinds of condition your plants live in but we guarantee it will be worth it in the end!

Water quantity

Plants – all plants want a different amount of water. Some like to dry out between watering and some need to stay moist at times. Always ask the store you bought the plant from what care it needs. Sometimes the plant comes with a little note with sun and water indications, usually a scale from zero to five. Lower drops of water mean less amount of water or longer time between watering. More drops mean more often or bigger quantities of water each time.
Usually, plants like succulents, hoyas, sansevierias and cacti thrive with being watered less, but still frequently. As opposed to calatheas, ferns, aglaonemas and some tradescantia who prefer to keep pretty moist.


Water quality

Depending on where you live, the water in your tap may contain a lot or close to none of chemicals. These are usually no problem for us humans, but for plants they can over a long time (or directly!) harm your plant’s foliage and roots. Most calatheas and fly-traps are sensitive to the calcium in our tap water and develop crispy and brown leaves. To battle this one either have to buy bottled water, get a filtering can that filters out calcium or collect rain water. You could also fill your watering can, let it sit for 24 hours and then water with everything except the last bit of water in the bottom, due to calcium sinking to the bottom over time.


Seasonal change

Depending on what season it is the amount of water a plant can change or remain the exact the same, it all depends on the conditions you live in. Is there a great temperature change? When the weather gets colder plants tend to need less water. Some plants even go into dormancy and require extremely little or no water at all. If you were to water for example a caladium rhizome it could molder due to excess water.

Greatest tip(!) in the world!

The best tip we could give you, or accually you already have the tip, all you need to do is to put it to use. We are of course talking about your finger tips! Whenever you are unsure of when you should water or not, just stick your finger in the soil. It the soil sticks to your finger then it's wet enough. If you finger is dry after putting in the soil then you can water it. Remeber to always stick you finger in the pot at least a third of the pots height. That way you are not just checking the top soil as that could be dry but the bottom could be soaking wet.
