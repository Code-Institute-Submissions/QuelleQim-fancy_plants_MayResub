The Value Of Correct Soil

Not every plant loves the standard store-bought soil, actually most plants don’t. So, don’t go crazy and repot all your plants with the same medium without checking first that the soil would be something the plant would like or even tolerate. Some plants like succulent have their special super draining soil they prefer. And some plants like orchids have special orchid bark. The same with mediterranean plants.


Soil Composition

Standard store-bought soil in a bag is usually very heavy, compact and retains a lot of water. To some plants this is heaven, but to most it surely is not. Most plants like a bit of aeration. Air helps the roots grow stronger and reduces the chance of moldering. There are different ways to give more air to the roots without digging holes in the soil. Like mixing you soil with other helping mediums.


Perlite

Perlite is a heated volcanic mineral that has great aeration qualities and is also very light in weight. The medium resembles styrofoam and is a hollow rock-like mineral. It sucks up excess water and releases it over time, the same with air. This medium is greatly used in gardening indoors as well as outdoors in lawns due to grass is high in demand for aeration of the roots.


Coco Coir

Another option for aeration and lightening of the soil is coconut coir. Coconut coir is the middle fibrous coat of the coconut fruit. The coir is ground up to make the small millimeter sized bits and are due to its size and weight easy to use in planting. It’s a lot like perlite, but smaller and brown hued. Coconut coir retains moisture but quickly releases it, making it harder to overwater your plants. A downside to using coconut coir is that the coir contains no nutrins on its own like soil does. Which means you have to add nutrients somehow, either via liquid or slow releaze fertilization pellets.


Fancy Pants Potting Soil Mix

Brace yourselves, because we are about to spill the beans on our popular fancy soil mixture! Since we love plants and want everybody to love plants as much as we do, we decided to tell the world about our successful potting soil mixture that works for most plants that we have indoor. Are you ready? Here it comes:

  • 2 parts soil
  • 1 part perlite
  • 1 part coconut coir
Mix all the ingredient together and repot you lovely plants that either need a bigger pot or some fresh nutrients. After about 2-3 months you should give the soil some nutrients with either a liquid or slow release fertilizer.
